Below are frequently asked questions, you may find the answer for yourself

How often should I get my vehicle serviced for routine maintenance?

Regular maintenance is crucial for the longevity and performance of your vehicle. Generally, follow the manufacturer's recommendations outlined in your owner's manual. Common intervals include oil changes every 3,000 to 5,000 miles and comprehensive inspections every 12,000 miles or annually.


What signs indicate that my car needs immediate attention or repair?

Watch out for warning lights on your dashboard, unusual noises, changes in handling, or any fluid leaks. If you notice any of these signs, it's advisable to bring your vehicle in for inspection promptly to prevent further damage.


How do I choose the right tires for my vehicle, and when should I replace them?

Consider factors such as your vehicle type, driving conditions, and climate when selecting tires. Check the tread depth regularly, and replace tires when the tread wears down to 2/32 of an inch or if you notice uneven wear. Additionally, replace tires every six years, even if the tread looks adequate.


What steps can I take to extend the lifespan of my vehicle and prevent major issues?

Regular maintenance is key to ensuring your vehicle's longevity. Follow the manufacturer's recommended service schedule, including oil changes, fluid checks, and brake inspections. Pay attention to any unusual sounds or warning lights and address them promptly. Additionally, practice safe driving habits, such as avoiding aggressive driving and keeping your tires properly inflated, to minimize wear and tear. By staying proactive and addressing issues early, you can significantly extend the life of your vehicle and reduce the likelihood of major repairs.
